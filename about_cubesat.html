Cubesat Capabilities

Tiny satellite. Big missions. From sensing our atmosphere to talking to a ground station, students learn by doing — like real mission teams.

Sense the Atmosphere

Measure temperature, humidity, pressure & air-quality with onboard sensors. Try this: compare schoolyard vs. classroom readings and graph micro-climates.

Snap Earth Images

Capture images, learn resolution & exposure, and classify land, cloud, and water pixels. New skill: basic computer vision for real satellite photos.

Send Space Messages

Relay short text messages via satellite path and decode them on the ground. Wow moment: message your friend in another city through the sky.

Predict Orbits & Passes

Use real Two-Line Elements (TLEs) to predict when satellites fly overhead. Challenge: plan a ground contact window to the minute.

Point & Stabilize

Read gyros & magnetometers, simulate a reaction wheel, and keep the camera steady. Skill: closed-loop control — the heart of spacecraft.

what students will learn

Start simple, think like mission control. Each week adds a new system — from sensors and code to radio links and orbits.

Electronics & Sensors

IMU, GPS, temperature, barometer, and camera modules — wiring, safety, and clean data logging. Try this: log classroom vs. playground temps and plot your own micro-climate.

Code & Autonomy

Read sensors, trigger actions, and run timed “mission scripts” that make your satellite act on its own. Try this: auto-snap a photo when light crosses a threshold.

Radio & Ground Station

Learn how messages travel through the sky. Antennas, modulation basics, and decoding signals on the ground. Try this: send a short message and decode it with your “mini mission control.”
